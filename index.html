Higher School From MPBSE

New Pithampur Public H.S. School (2021-2022)

88.00%

The period from 2020 to 2022, during the COVID-19 pandemic in India, was a significant time of growth and learning for me. As a teenager experiencing the transition to adulthood, I navigated a range of emotions, from curiosity about love, renunciation to the realization of life's challenges, especially when my father tested positive for COVID-19 and was hospitalized. The 11th grade was particularly challenging, as all classes were held online, but exams were conducted offline, resulting in my lowest academic performance with a score of 68.4%. The poor internet connectivity and the distractions of online learning made it difficult to focus on my studies.
In the 12th grade, I tried to regain my focus, but the lack of a strong foundation in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics meant that I only concentrated on passing the board exams, without preparing for competitive exams like the JEE. Recognizing my own limitations, I decided to pursue coding after high school. Despite not securing admission to prestigious institutions like IITs or NITs, I joined Medi-Caps University, supported by my parents' belief in my potential and the opportunity it provided. This experience has been a valuable lesson in resilience and self-improvement.

High School From MPBSE

New Pithampur Public H.S. School (2019-2020)

91.33%

I was born in Rewa, Madhya Pradesh, and in January 2008, my family relocated to Pithampur, Dhar, in the same state. Throughout my childhood, I have always been a good student, something I attribute to the blessings of Saraswati Maa. I completed my early schooling at Shiva Academy E.M. School up to the 5th grade, and then continued my education at NPPS from the 6th grade onwards. In the 2019-20 academic year, I set a goal to be among the top 10 students in the MPBSE board examinations in Madhya Pradesh. However, I didn't put in as much effort as needed to reach that goal. Despite this, I achieved a commendable 91.33% and was the second topper in my school. Interestingly, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, MPBSE conducted all the exams except for the two language subjects (English and Hindi), and the final results were declared based on the completed exams.
